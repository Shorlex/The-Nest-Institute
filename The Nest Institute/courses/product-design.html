Unit 1

Introduction to Product Management and Development

Product management is the process of bringing a new product to market or improving an existing one. It begins with a notion for a product with which a consumer would engage and finishes with an assessment of the product's success. At The Nest Institute, hyou will learn the three major areas of product management, which are product discovery, product planning and product development.

Concepts




User Needs

'User needs' are the requirements that a service must meet in order for the user to receive the best possible outcome. Learn how to identify and implement user needs.



Market Analysis

Market analysis is the process of investigating the market in order to comprehend the dangers and possibilities, as well as how prospects and customers will react to your products or services. Learn the four types of market analysis such as surveys, interviews, focus groups and customer observation.



Understanding Customers

Customers understanding is all about learning all you need to know about your customers, such as their requirements, pain spots, jobs they are attempting to perform, and so on, as well as their existing experiences, in order to give the experience they anticipate in the future. Learn the four main customers need, which are price, quality, choice and convenience.

Unit 2

Minimum Viable Product(MVP)

The Minimum Viable Product, or MVP, is a development strategy in which a new product is brought to the market with minimal characteristics that are sufficient to capture the attention of customers. Only after receiving adequate input from the product's early consumers is the completed product launched to the market. At The Nest Institute, you will be taught the basic priciples of MVP and how to build MVP for product management.

Concepts



Design Thinking

When addressing and solving a problem, a designer employs a set of cognitive processes and maybe design methodologies. Learn the three main elements of design thinking, which are empathy, expansive thinking and experimentation.



Product Matrics

Product metrics are quantifiable data points that a business tracks and analyzes to gauge the success of its product. Learn the four main types of matrics, which are Viewership Matrics, Engagement Matrics, Lead-Based Matrics and Sales Matrics.

Unit 3

Product Documentation

Product documentation is a sort of technical documentation that describes practically everything there is to know about a product or piece of software. The purpose of product documentation is to enable consumers to get the most out of the product in question. Unit 3 will expose you to various types of product documentation and how to create product documentation.

Concepts



Understanding Technology

With the use of technology, product management and development becomes easier. You will the the basic technicals skills that a product manager should have.



Working with Stakeholders

stakeholder can be any person or group of people who have an interest in the product and its success, who can influence product decisions, who are impacted (directly or indirectly) by the product. You will lwaern the various types of stakeholders and who to work effectively with them.

Upon completion of Product Design Course, project shall be given to you to demostrate your newly learnt skill. The instructor will give adequate guidance whenever necessary during the course of the project.
